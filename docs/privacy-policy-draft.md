## Information We Collect

StockLog collects information needed to operate organization-based inventory, account, invoice, payment, reporting, and onboarding workflows.

### Account Information

We collect account information such as user name, email address, login credentials managed through authentication services, role, status, and related profile details.

### Organization/Ranch Information

We collect organization or ranch information such as organization name, setup details, user membership, roles, and settings used to run StockLog for that organization.

### Inventory/Product Information

We collect inventory and product information such as product names, categories, SKUs, quantities, unit labels, prices, vendors, stock adjustments, and inventory activity.

### Customer/Account Information

Business/customer data may include customer or account names, phone numbers, email addresses, billing addresses, notes, K2 account records, and related activity.

### Invoice and Payment Records

StockLog may store invoice, statement, payment, balance, payment method label, reference number, and related recordkeeping notes. StockLog does not intentionally collect payment card numbers or process card payments directly.

### Invite and User-Role Data

StockLog uses one-time invite codes for onboarding. It may store invitation status, invited email, invited role, expiration, acceptance details, and user-role data. Plain invite codes should only be shown during the onboarding handoff.

### Technical, App Usage, and Security Logs

StockLog and its service providers may collect technical logs needed for authentication, app operation, troubleshooting, security, abuse prevention, and performance monitoring. This may include device/browser information, timestamps, IP-derived security metadata, and error details.

## How We Use Information

We use collected information to:

- Provide StockLog app functionality.
- Manage inventory, products, accounts, invoices, statements, payments, reports, and activity history.
- Authenticate users and maintain secure sessions.
- Enforce organization membership, user roles, and permissions.
- Support invite onboarding and account setup.
- Provide support and troubleshoot issues.
- Monitor security, prevent misuse, and investigate errors or unauthorized access.
- Comply with legal, tax, accounting, or business recordkeeping obligations where applicable.

## Data Sharing

StockLog does not sell personal data.

We may share or process information with:

- Supabase and other cloud service providers that provide authentication, database, hosting, logging, or infrastructure services.
- Authorized users within the same organization according to their roles and permissions.
- Support or operations personnel when needed to provide support, troubleshoot issues, or maintain the service.
- Legal, regulatory, or compliance parties if required by law, court order, or valid legal process.

## Security

StockLog uses email/password authentication and organization-scoped access controls. User roles are intended to limit access to sensitive actions and records.

Security practices include:

- Authentication through Supabase.
- Role-based access controls.
- Organization-scoped data access.
- One-time invite onboarding codes.
- Service-role secrets kept out of the frontend app.
- Local/admin-controlled private exports that should not be committed to source control.

No system can guarantee perfect security. Users should use strong passwords, protect invite codes, limit account access to authorized personnel, and report suspected unauthorized access promptly.

- Request correction of inaccurate business or customer records.
- Request export of organization data.
- Request deletion or deactivation of a user account.
- Contact support for help with access, data, or troubleshooting.

Contact support or the StockLog operator to make privacy, export, correction, or deletion requests.

## Children’s Privacy

StockLog is intended for business and ranch operations. It is not intended for children and does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact us so we can review and take appropriate action.
